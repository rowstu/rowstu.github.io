Removing Irrationality from Denominators

Example 1: Simplify 9 / 3√6

1. Start with the original expression:

   9
 ─────
 3√6

2. Simplify by canceling the 3:

  9       3
───── = ────
3√6      √6

3. Rationalize the denominator by multiplying by √6/√6:

  3      √6     3√6
──── × ──── = ─────
 √6     √6      6

4. Simplify the result:

 3√6     √6
──── = ────
  6      2

Example 7: Simplify 3 / (4 - √6) using Conjugates

When the denominator contains a binomial with a square root, multiply by the conjugate.

1. Multiply by the conjugate (4 + √6)/(4 + √6):

    3        4 + √6       3(4 + √6)
──────── × ──────── = ─────────────────
 4 - √6     4 + √6     (4 - √6)(4 + √6)

2. Simplify the denominator using difference of squares:

(4 - √6)(4 + √6) = 4² - (√6)² = 16 - 6 = 10

3. Expand the numerator:

3(4 + √6) = 12 + 3√6

4. Final answer:

12 + 3√6       6     3√6
──────── = ──── + ─────
   10        5      10
