To simplify \( \frac{9}{3\sqrt{6}} \) and remove the irrationality in the denominator, follow these steps:

  1. Start with the original expression:

    \[ \frac{9}{3\sqrt{6}} \]

  2. Simplify the fraction by canceling the \( 3 \):

    \[ \frac{9}{3\sqrt{6}} = \frac{3}{\sqrt{6}} \]

  3. Rationalize the denominator by multiplying both numerator and denominator by \( \sqrt{6} \):

    \[ \frac{3}{\sqrt{6}} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{6}} = \frac{3\sqrt{6}}{6} \]

  4. Simplify the resulting fraction:

    \[ \frac{3\sqrt{6}}{6} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2} \]

Thus, the rationalized form is:

\[ \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2} \]

To simplify \( \frac{2}{4\sqrt{6}} \), follow these steps:

  1. Simplify the fraction \( \frac{2}{4} \):

    \[ \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2} \]

    Now the expression becomes:

    \[ \frac{1}{2\sqrt{6}} \]

  2. Rationalize the denominator by multiplying both the numerator and denominator by \( \sqrt{6} \):

    \[ \frac{1}{2\sqrt{6}} \times \frac{\sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{6}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2 \times 6} \]

  3. Simplify the denominator:

    \[ 2 \times 6 = 12 \]

    So the fraction becomes:

    \[ \frac{\sqrt{6}}{12} \]

Thus, the simplified form is:

\[ \frac{\sqrt{6}}{12} \]

To simplify \( \frac{14}{\sqrt{2}} \), follow these steps:

    \[ \frac{14\sqrt{2}}{2} \]

  3. Simplify the fraction \( \frac{14\sqrt{2}}{2} \):

    Divide both the numerator and denominator by 2:

    \[ \frac{14\sqrt{2}}{2} = 7\sqrt{2} \]

Thus, the simplified form is:

\[ 7\sqrt{2} \]

To simplify \( \frac{9}{2\sqrt{3}} \), follow these steps:

  1. Rationalize the denominator by multiplying both the numerator and denominator by \( \sqrt{3} \):

    \[ \frac{9}{2\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{9\sqrt{3}}{2 \cdot \sqrt{3} \cdot \sqrt{3}} \]

  2. Simplify the denominator:

    Since \( \sqrt{3} \cdot \sqrt{3} = 3 \), the denominator becomes \( 2 \cdot 3 = 6 \). The fraction is now:

    \[ \frac{9\sqrt{3}}{6} \]

  3. Simplify the fraction:

    Divide the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor, \( 3 \):

    \[ \frac{9\sqrt{3}}{6} = \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \]

Thus, the simplified form is:

\[ \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \]

Removing Irrationality from the Denominator of \( \frac{3}{4 - \sqrt{6}} \)

To remove the square root from the denominator, follow these steps:

  1. Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator The conjugate of \( 4 - \sqrt{6} \) is \( 4 + \sqrt{6} \). Multiply both numerator and denominator by this:

\[ \frac{3}{4 - \sqrt{6}} \cdot \frac{4 + \sqrt{6}}{4 + \sqrt{6}} = \frac{3(4 + \sqrt{6})}{(4 - \sqrt{6})(4 + \sqrt{6})} \]

  1. Simplify the denominator using the difference of squares formula The denominator becomes:

\[ (4 - \sqrt{6})(4 + \sqrt{6}) = 4^2 - (\sqrt{6})^2 = 16 - 6 = 10 \]

  1. Simplify the numerator Multiply \( 3 ) by ( 4 + \sqrt{6} \):

\[ 3(4 + \sqrt{6}) = 12 + 3\sqrt{6} \]

  1. Combine the results The fraction becomes:

\[ \frac{12 + 3\sqrt{6}}{10} \]

  1. Simplify further by factoring Factor \( 12 + 3\sqrt{6} \) in the numerator. Both terms share a common factor of \( 3 \):

\[ \frac{12 + 3\sqrt{6}}{10} = \frac{3(4 + \sqrt{6})}{10} \]

Now simplify by dividing the \( 3 \) and \( 10 \) through their greatest common divisor, \( 2 \):

\[ \frac{3(4 + \sqrt{6})}{10} = \frac{6}{5} + \frac{\sqrt{6}}{5} \]

Therefore, The simplified expression is:

\[ \frac{6}{5} + \frac{3\sqrt{6}}{10} \]
