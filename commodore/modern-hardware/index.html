Modern Hardware for the Commodore 64

Home / Browse/ Commodoremodern Hardware

The C64 scene is alive and well. A whole ecosystem of modern hardware exists to keep these machines useful and enjoyable. This is a rundown of the most important upgrades.

Video Output

The original C64 outputs composite and S-Video (via the 8-pin DIN). Getting a clean picture on a modern display is the first problem to solve.

RetroTINK 2X / 5XAnalogue-to-HDMI upscalerThe gold standard. Low latency, excellent picture. 5X does 1080p with scanline options

Commodore monitor (1702/1084)Original CRTBest picture if you can find one. S-Video input gives a sharp image

Recommendation: S-Video cable + RetroTINK for modern TVs, or a 1084S monitor if you want the authentic look.

Storage Solutions

Floppy drives are slow and disks are unreliable after 40 years. Modern storage is essential.

SD Card Based

DeviceInterfaceNotes
SD2IECIEC serial busCheapest option (~$25). Plugs into the serial port. Reads D64/D81/PRG files from SD card. Not 100% compatible with fast loaders — works for ~90% of software
Pi1541IEC serial busRaspberry Pi-based cycle-exact 1541 emulation. Near-perfect compatibility. Needs a Pi Zero/3 and a small PCB
1541 Ultimate II+Cartridge portThe ultimate solution. Full 1541 emulation, REU, GeoRAM, ethernet, audio out. Expensive but nothing beats it

Cartridge Based

DeviceNotes

Recommendation: SD2IEC to get started, Pi1541 for better compatibility, 1541 Ultimate II+ if you’re serious.

Audio

The C64’s SID chip (6581 or 8580) is legendary, but the internal speaker amp is mediocre.

UpgradeNotes
SIDKickFPGA-based SID replacement. Emulates both 6581 and 8580 with switchable filters
SwinSIDCheaper SID replacement using a microcontroller. Not cycle-exact but decent
ARMSIDARM-based SID replacement. Good compatibility
Audio-out modTap the SID’s audio output pin and route to a 3.5mm jack. Bypasses the internal amplifier for much cleaner sound

Power Supply

This is critical. Original C64 power supplies (the black brick) are known to fail and send unregulated voltage to the motherboard, killing chips.

ReplacementNotes
Ray Carlsen PSUHand-built, properly regulated, considered the best. Limited availability
Electroware C64 PSUModern switchmode replacement. Good quality, readily available
C64 SaverInline voltage monitor. Cuts power if the 5V rail goes over ~5.4V. Cheap insurance for original PSUs

Never use an untested original PSU on a working C64. At minimum, measure the 5V DC rail with a multimeter — it should read 5.0-5.2V. Anything over 5.4V and the PSU is failing.
