plenipotentiary - a person, especially a diplomat, invested with the full power of independent action on behalf of their government, typically in a foreign country.

pluralist -

  1. an advocate of a system in which two or more states, groups, principles, sources of authority, etc., coexist. “religious pluralists”
  2. a member of the clergy who holds more than one ecclesiastical office at a time: “today many Anglican clergy in rural areas are technically pluralists”

catechism - a summary of the principles of Christian religion in the form of questions and answers, used for religious instruction.

vassalage - a position of subordination or submission (as to a political power).

rebarbative - unattractive and objectionable.

ribidinous - showing excessive sexual drive; lustful.

ordure - excrement; dung. something regarded as vile or abhorrent.

mendacity - the quality of being mendacious; untruthfulness; tendency to lie.

proclivity - a tendency to choose or do something regularly; an inclination or predisposition towards a particular thing.

immiscible - (of liquids) not forming a homogeneous mixture when mixed: “two immiscible liquids”

profligacy - reckless extravagance or wastefulness in the use of resources.

plainchant - Plainsong or plainchant (calque from the French plain-chant; Latin: cantus planus) is a body of chants used in the liturgies of the Western Church. When referring to the term plainsong, it is those sacred pieces that are composed in Latin text.

pugnascious - eager or quick to argue, quarrel, or fight.

aphorisms - a pithy observation which contains a general truth.

hussar - A hussar was a member of a class of light cavalry, originally from the Kingdom of Hungary during the 15th and 16th centuries.

cromach - crooked or bent person.

otiose - serving no practical purpose or result: “there were occasions when I felt my efforts were rather otiose”

grandilquent - pompous or extravagant in language, style, or manner, especially in a way that is intended to impress.

peroration - the concluding part of a speech, typically intended to inspire enthusiasm in the audience: “he again invoked the theme in an emotional peroration”

paeans - a song of praise or triumph.

demurred - raise objections or show reluctance: “normally she would have accepted the challenge, but she demurred”

hegemony - leadership or dominance, especially by one state or social group over others: “Germany was united under Prussian hegemony after 1871”

sinecures - a position requiring little or no work but giving the holder status or financial benefit: “political sinecures for the supporters of ministers”

mephistophelian - wicked; fiendish: “a Mephistophelian cackle.” The word comes from a demonic character in German folklore, Mephistopheles, whose name stems from mephitz, “destroyer” in Hebrew, and tophel, “liar.”

ermines - fur resembling ermine but with white spots on a black ground.

gerrymander - manipulate the boundaries of (an electoral constituency) so as to favour one party or class.

furtive - attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt or a belief that discovery would lead to trouble; secretive: “they spent a furtive day together”
