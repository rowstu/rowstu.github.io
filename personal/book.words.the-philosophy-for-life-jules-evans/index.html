Words from The Philosophy For Life by Jules Evans

libertine - an extreme form of hedonism or liberalism. Libertines put value on physical pleasures, meaning those experienced through the senses.

bucolic - relating to the pleasant aspects of the countryside and country life.

farrago - a jumble or medley of disorganized, haphazard, or even nonsensical ideas or elements.

tarry - you stay there longer than you meant to and delay leaving.

languid - having or showing a disinclination for physical exertion or effort.

calumny - the making of false and defamatory statements about someone in order to damage their reputation; slander.

conflagration - an extensive fire which destroys a great deal of land or property.

vicissitudes - a change of circumstances or fortune, typically one that is unwelcome or unpleasant.

incorrigible - (of a person or their behaviour) not able to be changed or reformed. “he’s an incorrigible liar”

pernicious - having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way.

extirpate - eradicate or destroy completely.

usary - the action or practice of lending money at unreasonably high rates of interest.

babel - a confused noise made by a number of voices.

teleological - relating to or involving the explanation of phenomena in terms of the purpose they serve rather than of the cause by which they arise.
