Key lessons from Don’t Believe Everything You Think

Thinking is an active process.

Thoughts happen all by them selves.

The two are similar but not the same thing.

Stopping thinking takes active effort and you quickly fatigue.

Thoughts unchecked will happen all by themselves.

A simpler approach as prescribed by meditative philosophies is to simply watch the thoughts and do you best to not engage in thinking about the thoughts.

Your active thinking muddies the existing water of thoughts.

Let the background thoughts come and go. This is the same as what happens when you are in the “flow” state. When in the flow state, you don’t think and add extra drama to your life. You are engaged in your task at hand and nothing more. Time stops and you are by definition happy.
