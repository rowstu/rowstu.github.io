Notes on the book Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi

The book explores the concept of “flow,” which is a state of mind characterized by total immersion in an activity.

In flow, people typically feel a sense of control, loss of self-consciousness, and enjoyment.

The book says that people are happiest when they are in a state of flow, and that achieving flow is essential for leading a fulfilling life.

Flow relates to creativity and problem-solving.

Flow can be cultivated and integrated into everyday life.

The key elements of the flow state as having clear goals, immediate feedback, a balance of challenge and skills, and a deep sense of concentration or absorption in the activity at hand.

Increase the chances of finding flow in daily life, by setting personal goals, developing skills and finding challenging activities to engage in.
