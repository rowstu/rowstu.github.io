Notes on the book The Four Agreements by Don Miguel Ruiz

The presents a code of conduct based on ancient Toltec wisdom. The agreements are principles aimed at achieving personal freedom and living a more fulfilling life.

  • Be impeccable with your word: This agreement emphasizes the power of language and encourages speaking with integrity. It means avoiding gossip, speaking truthfully, and using your words to spread love and kindness rather than negativity.

  • Don’t take anything personally: This agreement teaches that what others say and do is a reflection of their own reality, not yours. It encourages you not to take things personally or to allow the opinions and actions of others to affect your self-worth.

  • Don’t make assumptions: Making assumptions can lead to misunderstandings and unnecessary suffering. This agreement encourages clear communication and asking questions when in doubt rather than assuming the intentions or thoughts of others.

  • Always do your best: This agreement acknowledges that your best effort will vary from moment to moment and encourages you to simply do your best in any given situation. By doing so, you can avoid self-judgment and regret, knowing that you’ve done all you could at that moment.

The agreements serve as a guide for personal growth, emotional well-being, and spiritual enlightenment, helping you to live authentically and find peace within themselves and their relationships.
